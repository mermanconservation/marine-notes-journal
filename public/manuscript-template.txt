SPECIAL GUIDELINES FOR FIELD NOTES & OBSERVATIONAL REPORTS:

FIELD NOTES FORMAT:
- Date and time of observation
- Geographic location (GPS coordinates, depth if applicable)
- Observer name(s) and qualifications
- Environmental conditions (temperature, visibility, sea state, weather)
- Species identification (scientific name, common name)
- Behavioral observations or phenomena observed
- Photographic/video documentation (if available)
- Equipment used for observation and documentation
- Brief discussion of significance or unusual aspects

OBSERVATIONAL REPORTS FORMAT:
1. INTRODUCTION
   [Context and significance of the observation]

2. OBSERVATION DETAILS
   2.1 Location and Time
       [Precise geographic coordinates, dates, duration]
   2.2 Methods
       [Observation techniques, equipment, sampling protocol]
   2.3 Environmental Conditions
       [Water temperature, salinity, weather, sea state, habitat description]
   2.4 Observer Information
       [Qualifications, experience, number of observers]

3. OBSERVATIONS
   [Detailed description of phenomena, species, or events observed]
   [Include measurements, counts, behavioral descriptions]

4. DISCUSSION
   [Comparison with existing literature]
   [Ecological or conservation significance]
   [Potential implications]

5. CONCLUSIONS
   [Summary of key observations and their importance]

REQUIRED ELEMENTS FOR BOTH:
- High-quality photographs with scale bars where applicable
- GPS coordinates with datum specified (e.g., WGS84)
- Species identification keys or references used
- Any corroborating evidence or witness accounts
- Declaration of observation accuracy and limitations
